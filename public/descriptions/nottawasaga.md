## Details

The upper Nottawasaga through the Hockley Valley is a spring quick-water run: continuous current, tight corners, and gravel swifts winding under the hills between Orangeville and Alliston. It's a snowmelt-and-rain river — up for a few weeks in March–April and after big storms, gone by summer. Lower down (Nicolston to Baxter/Angus, the old canoe-race stretch) the river is bigger and slower, a good early-season tour when everything else is flooding.

## Levels

No published thresholds for this stretch; bands are scaled from the Hockley gauge record (typical spring peak ≈ 18 m³/s, summer low under 1). If the Hockley gauge is climbing past 5 m³/s, the valley section is moving; the window closes fast on the recession.

## Hazards

Sweepers and strainers are the story here — a narrow river in a wooded valley collects deadfall every winter. Scout blind corners, and expect to portage new wood.
