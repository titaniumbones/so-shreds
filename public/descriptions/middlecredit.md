## Details

The middle Credit from Terra Cotta down through Glen Williams to Norval is the valley's moving-water tour: continuous current, gravel swifts, and a couple of quick maneuvers where the river narrows above Terra Cotta and again through the Glen. It's the natural next step after the flatwater sections — Class I with a few II-ish moments at spring flows, and about 8 km (under 1.5 hours) from Glen Williams to Norval.

The **Glen Williams play spot** by the Main St / Mountainview Rd bridge offers a friendly surf at the right levels, with easy parking in the park on the southeast side of the bridge.

## Access

- **Put-in:** the small park/playground in Terra Cotta beside King St, or start in Glen Williams at the bridge for the shorter lower half.
- **Take-out:** Willow Park Ecology Centre on the west bank at Norval, at the Silver Creek confluence — **before the Norval Dam**. The courts have sided with the landowner: portaging the dam is trespassing. Do not miss this take-out.

## Levels

No published thresholds for this stretch; the bands here are scaled from the Boston Mills gauge record (typical spring peak ≈ 30 m³/s, summer low ≈ 2). Spring and after sustained rain is the season — by mid-summer it's a scrape.
