## Details

The Ottawa at Beachburg (Main and Middle channels around the islands below McCoys) is world-class big-water park-and-play: warm water, huge features, deep channels, and rapids that are runnable — in some form — every single day of the year. The Main channel carries the classic line through McCoys and the Lorne rapids (Garburator, Bus Eater); the Middle channel is the friendlier alternative with Butcher's Knife and Angel's Kiss.

Levels change the river completely rather than turning it on or off: certain waves come "in" only within narrow flow windows, which is why locals obsess over the daily number.

## Levels

Paddlers traditionally quote the Ottawa in feet on the Lorne gauge rather than m³/s; the reading shown here is the federal Britannia station well downstream, so treat it as a broad indicator. Low summer flows (under ~1500 m³/s at Britannia) mean friendly play levels; spring flood several times that brings out the huge-wave crowd and closes rafting sections.

## Hazards

Big volume means big holes and long swims. McCoys at high water is no joke. Commercial raft traffic on summer weekends.
