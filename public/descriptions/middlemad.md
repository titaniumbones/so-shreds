## Details

The middle Madawaska below Bark Lake Dam is the river Madawaska Kanu Centre built its campus on: a dam-release Class II–III playground of warm summer water, surf waves, and forgiving pools, running most days from late June through August when everything rain-fed has dried up. Staircase and the sets through the MKC property are the heart of it.

Because the flow is a scheduled release rather than weather, the day-of-week matters as much as the season — releases and levels can step up or down abruptly.

## Levels

There is no public numeric gauge on the section. The number shown here is the WSC Palmer Rapids station, well downstream: in summer it mostly reflects the Bark Lake release (with a lag of several hours), so treat it as an indicator, not gospel. The [Shaggy Designs ultrasonic sensor](https://shaggydesigns.com/gauge/madawaska.htm) sits on the section itself and is the most precise reading available.

## Hazards

Release changes mid-day can catch a parked boat or a lunch spot; tie things up. The dam itself and the first drop below it are off-limits.
