## Details

The Petawawa drains the high country of Algonquin Park and is the biggest natural-flow whitewater river in this part of Ontario. The town section near Petawawa (Rapides des Joachims-style big waves at flood) is the roadside run; the park sections (Lake Travers to McManus Lake) are a celebrated multi-day wilderness trip with Class II–IV rapids, best in May and June.

Being undammed, the Pet rises and falls with snowmelt and rain: huge in late April, often bony by August.

## Levels

Locals quote the town run as **gauge height in metres** on the WSC station: normal season is **2.8–4.0 m**, about **3.2 m is a good medium**, and the Catwalk wave comes in above **2.9 m**. (The painted feet gauge on the Hwy 17 bridge is an unrelated older scale.) In discharge terms 2.8 m ≈ 60 m³/s and 3.2 m ≈ 120 m³/s; spring peaks past 400 m³/s are serious, cold, big water for experienced crews only. The upper river in Algonquin runs roughly two days ahead of the town reading after rain.

## Hazards

Cold spring water, long swims in continuous sections, and real wilderness logistics inside the park — book access and know your exits. Rollway and The Natch have claimed boats at high water.
