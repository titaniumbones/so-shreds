## Details

The Magnetawan between Ahmic Harbour and Maple Island is the run the Kawartha Whitewater Paddlers page describes: five to seven substantial granite pool-drop rapids in the Class III–V range, headlined by the **Poverty Chutes** into Poverty Bay. Big pools separate the drops, but the rapids themselves are serious — scout, set safety, and know which lines are yours. (The wilderness canyon below Poverty Bay and the Thirty Dollar Rapids stretch near Britt are separate, longer expeditions.)

Snowmelt off the Almaguin highlands keeps it padded through May and June; by midsummer it's technical and bony.

## Levels

Per KWP: broadly runnable from about **7 m³/s up past 100**, with the sweet spot around **40–60**; the Feighan Falls put-in only works above ~60. The gauge is at the river mouth near Britt, well below the run — read it as a whole-river index rather than an on-site measurement.

Source: [KWP Magnetawan page](https://www.kwpclub.ca/rivers/magnetawan).

## Hazards

Class V consequences at the biggest chutes; portages are on rough Shield granite. Remote enough that a swim with lost gear is a real problem.
