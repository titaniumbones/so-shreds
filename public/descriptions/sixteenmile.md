## Details

Sixteen Mile Creek drops off the Niagara Escarpment and carves down through Oakville to Lake Ontario, and after a big rain or spring ice-out the middle valley turns into a genuine whitewater run — Class I–III depending on level. Expect tight, technical rapids where the creek pinches through red shale-limestone gorges, interspersed with big rollers and standing waves at high flows, and long meandering flatter stretches in between. Budget more time than the map suggests: the river winds constantly, so a modest distance is a 3–5 hour day.

This is a catch-it-when-it's-there creek. It runs only briefly — during spring melt or in the day or two after a heavy rain — and drops out fast.

## Access

- **Put-in:** Sixteen Valley Conservation Area, north Oakville, below where the Milton main branch and the East branch have joined.
- **Take-out:** downtown Oakville — the public canoe/kayak ramp under the Rebecca/Randall Street bridge, where the creek opens into the harbour marsh. Get out earlier if you only want the whitewater and not the flatwater paddle-out.

## Levels

No published gauge thresholds for this run. The bands are scaled from the **Sixteen Mile Creek at Milton** gauge (02HB005), which sits on the main branch *above* the run — the East branch (02HB004, near Omagh) is the larger tributary and joins upstream of the put-in, so the water actually under your hull is well above the Milton number. Watch both gauges: they rise together after rain, and this creek only comes up at big rainfall or ice-out.

**Hazard:** river-wide logs and strainers are frequent, and the creek changes with every high-water event. Scout blind corners and be ready to portage wood.
