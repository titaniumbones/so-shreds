## Details

The Lower Madawaska between Palmer Rapids and Griffith is the classic eastern Ontario club run: pool-drop Class II–III granite rapids through pine and rock-barrens country, with warm(ish) water and forgiving recovery pools. Aumonds Bay to Griffith is the most-travelled stretch; Snake Rapids is the biggest and most scouted set. Flow is moderated upstream by Bark Lake dam, so the river holds paddleable water well into summer most years.

The Palmer Rapids play park beside the village is a separate, shorter destination — good surf waves at a wide range of flows, and the usual venue for spring festivals and clinics.

## Levels

The gauge at Palmer Rapids reads the flow entering the lower river. Roughly: below ~15 m³/s things get bony; 25–110 m³/s is the sweet range (higher = pushier); spring flood above ~170 m³/s washes out surf spots and demands solid big-water skills.

## Hazards

Snake Rapids and Rifle Chute deserve a scout at unfamiliar levels. Remote enough that self-rescue matters: cell coverage is patchy between Palmer Rapids and Griffith.
